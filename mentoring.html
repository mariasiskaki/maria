Illinois Geometry Lab (IGL)

IGL - Uni High is a four-week summer project for talented University High School students. In the summer of 2019 was the mentor for the project "Numerical norm estimates for some classes of Harper type operators". The goal of the project was to generate models of the effects of magnetic fields on the energy states of electrons within atomic lattices. We analyzed three different types of lattices: square, triangular, and hexagonal. One application of our research is on the properties of graphene, a carbon allotrope with a hexagonal lattice. Graphene is an extremely durable material with high thermal and electrical conductivity. Here is a complete report of our project.

Summer Illinois Camp

SIM camp is a week-long math day camp for middle and high school students, hosted by University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign Department of Mathematics. In July 2021 I designed and taught the (online) course "The Continued Fraction Universe", which is a gentle introduction to continued fractions. We talked about different number systems, the euclidean division algorithm, approximation properties of continued fractions, and applications to real-life problems including construction of the gears in solar system models and prediction of leaf growth patterns in plants. Here are some activities I created on number systems of base other than 10, continued fractions and Euclid's algorithm , and leaf growth patterns in plants.
